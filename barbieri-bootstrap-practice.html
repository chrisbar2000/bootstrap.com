What is P.A.N.D.A.S?

Similar to that of Autoimmune Encephalitis, this is an autoimmune disease where your immune system attacks your brain. More in depth, the immune system misinterprets your brain cells or tissues for anti-bacteria. This leads to brain inflammation. Doctors or medical professionals can not explain why this happens. It is usually uncommon or rare.

This disease was first discovered by Dr. Susan Swedo in 1998. Technically back in 1995 was the first real case of P.A.N.D.A.S where doctors found that other people underwent serious changes from some disaease caused by strep infection. That was when Pediatric Autoimmune Neuropsychiatric Disorders Associated with Streptoccal was born. The first study. Tweleve years later, The P.A.N.S. Pediatric Acute-Onset Neuropsychiatric disease was discovered in 2010. This is another version of the disease.

This autoimmune disease can often go misdiagnosed by doctors or medical professionals. They don't necessarily understand why someone would change like that to that degree.
